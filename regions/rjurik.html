Region Overview

The Untamed North

The Rjurik Highlands stretch across the frozen north of Cerilia — a vast expanse of ancient forests, glacier-carved fjords, and snow-capped mountains. Here, the druids of Erik hold sway over a proud, independent people who measure wealth not in gold but in honor, cattle, and the strength of one's sworn word.

The Rjurik have never been conquered, and they never intend to be. Their jarls rule by the consent of their people, and any leader who forgets this will find himself ruling an empty hall.

Domains of Power

Notable Domains

Halskapa

The largest Rjurik realm, ruled by the Overseer of Halskapa. A constant struggle between civilization and the wild.

Stjordvik

A prosperous coastal realm that balances Rjurik tradition with trade. The jarl here walks a fine line between old ways and new wealth.

Rjuvik

The most warlike of the Rjurik realms. Its jarl believes strength alone should determine who rules the highlands.

Blood Skull Barony

An orcish domain that constantly threatens the southern Rjurik lands — a reminder that civilization's grip is tenuous here.

Hogunmark

A druid-led realm where Erik's followers hold more power than any jarl. The forest itself seems to protect this land.

Lore & Identity

Culture & Themes

Theme 01

The Old Ways

The druidic traditions of Erik run deeper than any political boundary. Nature worship permeates every aspect of Rjurik life — from the blessing of the harvest to the funeral rites performed under ancient oaks. Yet progress knocks at every door: Anuirean missionaries bring temples to Haelyn, merchants bring foreign goods and foreign ideas. The tension between preservation and progress defines the Rjurik soul. Those who cling too tightly to the old ways risk stagnation; those who abandon them risk losing everything that makes the Rjurik people who they are.

Theme 02

Blood and Honor

Personal valor is the currency of respect among the Rjurik. A man's word is his bond, and oath-breaking is considered the gravest of sins — worse than murder, for it corrupts the very fabric of trust that holds communities together. The Thing, or assembly, serves as the great equalizer: here, any free person may speak, any grievance may be aired, and justice is rendered by the collective will. When words fail, trial by combat settles the matter. The Rjurik do not see violence as barbaric — they see it as honest.

Theme 03

The Endless Winter

Survival is not a metaphor in the Highlands — it is the daily reality. The long dark of winter brings not just cold but isolation, madness, and the creatures that haunt the frozen wastes. Giants stalk the mountain passes. Wolves as large as horses prowl the deep woods. The White Witch's domain in the far north is a place where winter never ends, where the very land itself seems hostile to all warm-blooded life. To be Rjurik is to face nature at its most unforgiving and refuse to yield.
