█ The Echo
A DARK REFLECTION

The Shadow World is not a place — it is an echo. A dark reflection of Cerilia that exists just beyond the veil of reality, separated by a barrier called the Seeming. Here, everything is wrong. The sky is perpetually twilight, the colors are muted and sickly, and the air itself tastes of ash and despair.

The dead wander its twisted landscapes, unable to find rest, while darker things — creatures born of pure shadow — hunt anything foolish enough to cross over. Some scholars believe the Shadow World is what Cerilia would become if the god Azrai had won at Deismaar. Others say it's worse than that.

The Shadow World doesn't just reflect the real world — it remembers it. Every act of cruelty, every betrayal, every death leaves an imprint in the shadow, building a landscape of accumulated horror that grows stronger with each passing age.

█ 03
MECHANICS & LORE

How the Shadow World works at the table — and what it costs to go there.

Gameplay

CROSSING OVER

The Shadow World is not somewhere you simply walk into. The barrier between Cerilia and its dark mirror — the Seeming — must be breached. This can happen through shadow paths (rare, hidden trails that exist in both worlds simultaneously), powerful realm magic, or where the Seeming has worn dangerously thin. Some ancient battlefields and cursed lands offer permanent crossings. Every entry has a cost: the traveler must make a saving throw against shadow corruption, and the difficulty increases the longer they remain. The Shadow World wants visitors. It just doesn't want them to leave.

Horror

THE CORRUPTION

Those who linger in the Shadow World begin to change. First comes the cold — a chill that no fire can warm. Then the dreams, visions of the shadow leaking into waking hours. Personality shifts follow: paranoia, cruelty, emotional numbness. In gameplay terms, each day spent in the Shadow World requires a corruption check. Failed checks accumulate shadow taint that manifests as mechanical penalties and roleplay hooks. At its worst, a character loses themselves entirely, becoming a shadow creature — a hollow echo of who they once were, trapped in the dark mirror forever.

Deep Lore

THE HALFLING SECRET

The halflings of Cerilia carry a secret in their blood. Unlike every other race, they did not originate on Cerilia — they came from the Shadow World. Their ancestors fled that dark dimension long ago, crossing the Seeming to find refuge in the lands of the living. This origin gives halflings a unique and unsettling relationship with the shadow: they are partially resistant to its corruption, can sense thin points in the Seeming, and some can even walk the shadow paths without magic. But it also means the Shadow World remembers them — and it wants them back.
