■■ The Core of Birthright

THE BLOOD REMEMBERS

When gods died at Mount Deismaar, their divine essence scattered across the battlefield — flowing into mortal veins, creating the blooded scions who would shape the fate of Cerilia forever.

Read on Wiki

■ 01

THE BATTLE OF DEISMAAR

At the dawn of history, the gods themselves walked the earth alongside their chosen peoples. But the shadow god Azrai grew jealous and corrupted, gathering an army of monsters and dark followers to destroy his siblings and claim dominion over all creation. The other gods — Anduiras, Basaïa, Reynir, Brenna, Masela, and Vorynn — rallied their peoples for a final stand at Mount Deismaar, a volcanic island in the Straits of Aerele.

The battle was cataclysmic. Gods and mortals fought side by side against Azrai's horde. In the end, the gods realized they could not defeat Azrai through force alone. One by one, they sacrificed themselves, pouring their divine essence into a single devastating blow that shattered Azrai — but destroyed them all in the process.

As the gods died, their divine power had nowhere to go. It exploded outward across the battlefield, seeping into every warrior, every leader, every survivor who stood near the blast. These mortals absorbed fragments of godly power into their very blood. They became the first blooded scions — mortal vessels carrying shards of divine essence. This power would pass through their bloodlines for generations, granting supernatural abilities and, most importantly, the divine right to rule.

■ 02

THE SEVEN DERIVATIONS

Each bloodline traces back to one of the gods who fell at Deismaar. The derivation determines which blood abilities a scion might manifest.

Anduiras

Noble War

The bloodline of kings and warriors. Scions of Anduiras are natural leaders, blessed with courage and the divine authority to command. They feel the pull of duty and justice.

Blood Abilities: Divine Aura, Battlewise, Detect Lie, Leadership

Basaïa

Sun & Reason

The bloodline of scholars and priests. Basaïa's scions are gifted with wisdom and clarity, able to see truth where others see shadow. They are drawn to knowledge and justice.

Blood Abilities: Divine Wrath, Detect Illusion, True Seeing, Resistance

Reynir

Nature & Growth

The bloodline of druids and rangers. Reynir's blood runs green with the life force of the natural world. These scions feel the heartbeat of the earth itself.

Blood Abilities: Animal Affinity, Forest Walk, Healing, Nature's Embrace

Brenna

Commerce & Travel

The bloodline of merchants and rogues. Brenna's scions are silver-tongued and quick-witted, blessed with uncanny luck and the ability to read people like ledgers.

Blood Abilities: Detect Lie, Persuasion, Shadow Walk, Fortune

Masela

Sea & Storm

The bloodline of sailors and sea-lords. Masela's power surges like the tide — her scions command the waves, breathe the salt air, and navigate by instinct.

Blood Abilities: Sea Song, Weather Control, Water Breathing, Elemental Control

Vorynn

Moon & Magic

The bloodline of wizards and seers. Vorynn's scions are touched by arcane power, their blood humming with magical potential. They see the unseen and walk between worlds.

Blood Abilities: Mage Sense, Spell Turning, Divination, Shadow Sight

Azrai

Shadow & Corruption

The bloodline of the destroyer. Azrai's tainted blood is the most powerful — and the most dangerous. His scions are drawn to darkness, cruelty, and domination. Many become monsters.

Blood Abilities: Fear, Blood History, Shadow Form, Corruption

⚠ Warning: Scions of Azrai risk becoming Awnsheglien.

■ 03

BLOOD ABILITIES

Blood abilities manifest differently in each scion. The strength of your bloodline determines how many abilities you can wield — and how powerful they become. Bloodline strength ranges from Tainted (weakest) through Minor, Major, Great, to True (strongest — the power of a demigod).

Tainted

Strength 1

A trace of divine blood. May not even know they carry it.

Minor

Strength 2

Enough to feel the pull. One or two minor abilities.

Major

Strength 3

A true scion. Multiple abilities, recognized by the land itself.

Great

Strength 4

Power that makes kingdoms tremble. Rulers of nations.

True

Strength 5

Near-divine. The blood burns so bright it reshapes reality around the scion.

■ 04

THE AWNSHEGLIEN

The Blood Abominations

Not all scions use their power wisely. Those who carry Azrai's tainted blood — or who succumb to evil regardless of derivation — risk a terrible transformation. As corruption takes hold, the scion's body begins to warp, twisting into a monstrous form that reflects the darkness within. These abominations are called the Awnsheglien (awn-SHAY-lin), the "Blood of Darkness."

The Awnsheglien are among the most powerful and terrifying beings in Cerilia. Some were once heroes. Some were kings. All are now monsters — but monsters who remember what they were, which makes them far more dangerous than any mindless beast.

The Gorgon

Once a noble Anuirean prince, now a massive creature of living stone. The Gorgon is arguably the most powerful being in Cerilia, ruling the Crown of Anuire from his fortress with an army of monsters. His touch turns flesh to stone.

The Spider

A former elven queen consumed by Azrai's blood. She weaves webs of intrigue and literal spider-silk from her domain in the Spiderfell, plotting the downfall of every kingdom within reach.

The Sphinx

A Khinasi scholar who sought forbidden knowledge. Now a riddling monster who guards ancient secrets and devours those who answer incorrectly.

The Vampire

An Anuirean lord who made a pact with shadow. He haunts the borderlands between life and death, building an undead army in the darkness.

■ 05

THE ERSHEGHLIEN

The Blood of Light

For every Awnsheglien, there is an Ersheghlien (air-SHAY-lin) — a scion whose blood has transformed them in a positive way. These "Blood of Light" are beings of extraordinary power and beauty, shaped by the noblest aspects of their divine heritage. While rare, they serve as beacons of hope in a world threatened by darkness.

Where the Awnsheglien are twisted by corruption, the Ersheghlien are elevated by virtue. A scion of Reynir might become a living tree, an ancient guardian of the deepest forests. A scion of Masela might merge with the sea itself, an undying spirit of the deep waters. These transformations are not curses — they are ascensions, the blood reaching its fullest divine potential.

The Ersheghlien remind Cerilia that the gods' sacrifice was not in vain. The divine blood can illuminate as well as corrupt. The choice, as always, belongs to the scion.

■ 06

BLOODTHEFT

There is one way to seize another scion's power: bloodtheft. When a blooded scion is slain by a weapon forged of tighmaevril (enchanted bloodsilver), or by another blooded scion in personal combat, the killer absorbs the victim's bloodline strength. This is the darkest truth of Cerilia's politics — every blooded ruler is both a potential ally and a potential source of power. Assassinations are not merely political; they are harvests.

"This single mechanic is why Birthright's political game is so deadly. Trust no one. Guard your blood."
