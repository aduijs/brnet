■ Overview

Strength Takes
The Throne

Boeruine is the second most powerful domain in Anuire and the primary rival to Avanil for the Iron Throne. Where Prince Avan relies on diplomacy and alliances, Archduke Aeric Boeruine believes in the old ways — that the Iron Throne belongs to whoever has the strength to take it and hold it.

The Archduke commands the finest professional army in Anuire, trained in the harsh northern campaigns against goblin raiders and humanoid hordes. His soldiers are battle-hardened veterans, not parade-ground soldiers, and their loyalty to the Archduke is absolute. Boeruine's military tradition stretches back centuries, and every noble in the domain is expected to serve in the army before taking up any other duties.

Boeruine's claim to the throne rests not on lineage but on might. The Archduke's bloodline is powerful — one of the strongest in Anuire — and he has demonstrated time and again that he has the will and the capability to enforce his vision of a reunified empire. His critics call him a warmonger; his supporters call him the only man strong enough to restore order to a fractured continent.

"The Iron Throne was not built for diplomats. It was forged in battle, and it shall be claimed by steel."

— Archduke Aeric Boeruine

■ Key Figures

The Iron Circle

Archduke Aeric Boeruine

Ruler · Fighter · Blooded Scion

A towering figure both physically and politically, the Archduke is feared and respected in equal measure. He is blunt, direct, and utterly convinced that only military strength can reunify Anuire. His patience is legendary — but so is his temper.

Baron Gavin Tael

General · Northern Frontier Commander

The Archduke's most capable general and trusted lieutenant. Tael commands the northern frontier forces and has earned a fearsome reputation fighting goblin armies. His loyalty to Boeruine is absolute, forged through years of shared campaigns.

■ Political Landscape

War on Every Front

The Iron Throne Rivalry

The eternal struggle with Avanil defines Boeruine's foreign policy. While Prince Avan builds coalitions and courts allies, the Archduke prepares for the day when words will fail and only swords will decide the fate of the continent. Both domains know that open war between them would devastate Anuire — but neither is willing to yield their claim.

Critical Threat

Northern Defense

Constant warfare against humanoid threats from the Five Peaks keeps Boeruine's army sharp and battle-ready. Goblin raiders, orog war bands, and worse pour down from the mountains in seasonal waves. This perpetual state of conflict has forged the finest soldiers in Anuire — and gives the Archduke a standing army that no other domain can match.

Ongoing Conflict

The Talinie Question

Tensions with the neighboring theocracy of Talinie simmer constantly. The Thane of Talinie views Boeruine's military ambitions with deep suspicion, while the Archduke sees Talinie's religious authority as a challenge to his secular power. An uneasy border peace holds — for now.

■ Domain Power

Holdings & Might

Law

The Archduke holds extensive law holdings across his provinces. His authority is unquestioned within his borders — enforced by military garrisons.

Dominant

Temple

Temple holdings are shared between several churches. The worship of Haelyn dominates, though the Archduke keeps religious influence firmly subordinate to secular power.

Moderate

Guild

Guild activity is moderate. Trade flows through Seasedge, but the domain's focus on military matters means merchants are tolerated rather than courted.

Moderate

Source

Source holdings are limited. The Archduke has little interest in arcane matters, leaving magical sources largely unclaimed or contested by wandering wizards.

Sparse

Military Strength

Boeruine fields the finest professional army in all of Anuire. Battle-hardened from constant northern frontier campaigns, these are career soldiers — not levied peasants. The Archduke's forces include heavy infantry, armored cavalry, and siege engineers, all commanded by veteran officers who have earned their rank through blood.

A+ Combat Rating
