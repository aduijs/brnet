■ Overview

Where Two Worlds
Converge

Tuornen is a land of ancient forests, mysterious ruins, and old magic. Nestled in the heartlands of Anuire, it is one of the few domains where the bond between humans and elves remains strong. The domain's half-elven ruler, Gilgaed Fhiele, walks between two worlds — the human realm of politics and war, and the elven world of nature and ancient tradition.

The forests of Tuornen are among the oldest in Anuire, and they still harbor secrets from before the Battle of Deismaar. Ancient elven ruins dot the landscape, and the magical sources that flow through the land are unusually strong. This makes Tuornen a coveted prize for wizards and a constant target for neighboring domains that would exploit its resources.

Tuornen maintains its independence through a combination of forest warfare expertise, elven alliances, and the sheer difficulty of campaigning in its wooded terrain. The domain's soldiers are expert archers and guerrilla fighters who can vanish into the trees and strike from nowhere. Would-be conquerors have learned to their cost that Tuornen's forests fight back.

■ Figures of Note

Key Characters

Gilgaed Fhiele

Duke of Tuornen · Half-Elf Ranger

The half-elven Duke is torn between his human obligations as a ruler and his elven heritage. He is a skilled ranger and natural leader, but his dual nature makes him an outsider in both worlds. His bloodline of Reynir grants him a deep connection to the land itself, and the forests respond to his presence in ways that unnerve even his allies.

Ranger Reynir Bloodline Chaotic Good

Fhileraene

Elven Advisor · Voice of Tuarhievel

An elven advisor from Tuarhievel who serves as Gilgaed's connection to the elven court. She is ancient, wise, and deeply protective of the forests. Fhileraene has witnessed the slow decline of elven influence in Cerilia and sees in Tuornen a last chance to preserve something of the old world. Her counsel shapes much of the domain's forest policy.

Elven Elder Tuarhievel
■ Threats & Alliances

Political Landscape

01

The Alamie Aggression

Alamie has long pressed territorial claims on Tuornen's eastern provinces. The Duke of Alamie views the forests as rightfully his, a legacy of older borders drawn when the empire still stood. Border skirmishes are a seasonal occurrence, and full-scale war is never far from the horizon.

Conflict · Territory · War
02

The Elven Alliance

The relationship with Tuarhievel is Tuornen's greatest diplomatic asset — and its most delicate. The elves support Gilgaed because of his half-elven blood, but they have their own agenda regarding the forests. Maintaining this alliance requires constant negotiation between human progress and elven preservation.

Diplomacy · Elves · Balance
03

Forest Magic

Tuornen's rich magical sources are the envy of every wizard-regent in Anuire. The ley lines that run through its ancient forests are unusually potent, drawing the attention of source-holders both friendly and hostile. Controlling — or corrupting — these sources is a prize worth going to war over.

Magic · Sources · Power
■ Power Structure

Holdings & Assets

Sources

Strong

Tuornen's greatest asset. The ancient forests channel powerful magical ley lines, making source holdings among the richest in Anuire.

Law

Moderate

The Duke's law extends through settled areas but the deep forests remain largely ungoverned, following older elven customs and traditions.

Temples

Moderate

Erik's faith has a natural home here among the forest folk, while Haelyn's temples serve the more settled populations in the capital and towns.

The Forests

Critical

The forests themselves are Tuornen's greatest strategic asset. They hamper invading armies, shelter guerrilla fighters, and channel magical power.
